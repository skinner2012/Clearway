# Drafter κ baseline — an honest read

**What this is.** A per-class, chance-corrected measurement of whether the drafter is *judging* or
*stamping*, scored against W3C ACT expert gold — never against the judge. **Nothing here is scored by an
LLM**: every number is a deterministic replay of the checked-in run sweep. The frozen numbers live in
[`benchmark/reports/drafter_kappa_baseline.json`](../benchmark/reports/drafter_kappa_baseline.json) and are
regenerated by `uv run python -m clearway.eval.drafter_kappa_baseline` — no pipeline re-run. That command
asserts the three runs agree per class before freezing run_1 as canonical (the drafter is deterministic), and
records all three run ids, the drafter model digest and the ACT export hash as provenance. This baseline is
the reference a future drafter change is measured against; it is **not** a claim that the drafter is good.

**Why κ, not recall.** A constant classifier and a working instrument produce similar-looking recall.
Recall cannot separate "judged correctly" from "stamped one verdict and it happened to fit." κ can: it
is chance-corrected, so a rater with no variance scores 0 however the marginals fall.

## The four fix-unit classes

The unit is one ACT case (honest-misses carried in as CLEAN); the stratum is the **fix unit** (`axe_rule`),
so the two link rules pool into `link-name` — one shared missing referent, one future fix. The 2×2, CI and
ceiling are the headline reading (`partial_flags=True`); `κ (alt)` is the `partial_flags=False` reading.

| **empty-heading** *(control)* | 13 (5/8) | 4·1·1·7 | 0.85 | **0.675** | 0.675 | [0.156, 1.000] | 0.003 | 0.250 | No — control, must not move |

## The read

**`document-title` is a constant classifier — its flattering recall is an artifact.** It stamps FLAG on
all five cases (`fp=3, fn=0, tn=0`), so its recall reads 2/2 = perfect while it carries no discriminative
signal at all. κ = **0.000** exposes exactly this. Its bootstrap interval is **[0.000, 0.000]** — the
tightest number in the table, and a lie if read as precision: it is zero-width because the resampled
drafter stream is constant on **every** draw (`degenerate_share = 1.00`, `constant_classifier = true`),
i.e. *no variance because no signal*. This is the whole reason κ was pointed at the drafter.

**`empty-heading` is the control, and its positive κ is what proves the capability is real.** At the one
reachable referent the drafter actually discriminates: κ = **0.675** at 0.85 raw agreement, interval
strictly above zero (`[0.156, 1.000]`). Without a clearly-positive control, a table of low κ would be
indistinguishable from a broken instrument; this row is the evidence the instrument works. The artifact
reports `certifiable: false` for it (2 errors → best-case p = 0.25) — but that is *not a concern*: it is the
control, not a fix target, and **it must not move**. Its "certifiable" cell is read as n/a for that reason.

**Two classes have room to prove a future fix; `document-title` does not — at any fix quality.** The
ceiling is the one-sided exact sign-test p a *perfect* fix could reach (`p = 0.5^errors`, direction and
α = 0.05 pre-registered on the artifact before any fixed run exists). `link-name` (p = 0.002) and `label`
(p = 0.031) clear α. `document-title` cannot: with 3 errors the most generous attainable p is **0.125 >
0.05**, so no fix, however good, can reach significance here. That is a property of the **gold set's size
(n = 5)**, not of the drafter or of any future fix. Its eventual fix must be argued on *mechanism*
(constant stamp → discriminating, visible in the frozen verdict vector) and on its contribution to the
pooled false-positive rate, never on a per-class p-value.

**The second reading changes nothing that matters.** Under `partial_flags=False` only `link-name` moves
(κ 0.250 → 0.408, errors 9 → 7); every other class is identical and **no certifiability verdict flips**.

## What this baseline does not prove

The intervals are wide and are reported as observed — wide intervals honestly stated are the deliverable;
narrow ones would be the warning sign. It measures nothing about whether κ transfers from ACT's
**synthetic** pages to real audited ones (unmeasurable here), says nothing about remediation quality, and
describes only the current, known-unreliable drafter — it changes no drafter behaviour. Its authority
rests on honest method — external gold, deterministic replay, never LLM-scored, degeneracy disclosed,
ceilings stated — not on narrow intervals.
